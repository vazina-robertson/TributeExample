Andrei Andreyevich Markov

A mathematician, who made students go nuts after his HMM algorithm!

Below is Markov's life timeline:

  • 1856 - Born in Russia.
  • 1877 - Markov was awarded a gold medal for his work on Integration of Differential Equations by Continuous Fractions.
  • 1880 - Defended Master's thesis: "Binary Quadratic Forms with Positive Determinant."
  • 1885 - Defended Doctoral thesis: "Some Applications of Algebraic Continuous Fractions."
  • 1895 to 1905 - Lectured Differential Calculus.
  • 1886 - Was appointed as extraordinary professor.
  • 1890 - Became an extraordinary member of the academy.
  • 1908 - Removed from position due to Markov's refusal to monitor students.
  • 1912 - Protested on behalf of Leo Tolstoy to be excommunicated due to being atheist.
  • 1913 - Was elected as a honorary member of the University.
  • 1922 - Died in Russia.
